Editor's Letter

India is not just a market. It is a musical civilisation — one that is too complex, too alive, and too culturally specific to be served by any single editorial approach. This conversation is about how we see our role, what we're choosing to change, and where we're pointed. Not a strategy document. A point of view.

Unni Nambudripad
India Editorial Lead, Spotify
Conviction · 3 min read
We don't reflect culture.
We shape it.

What We Believe

Every editorial team has an opinion about what music is worth hearing. The question is whether that opinion is reactive or formative. India Editorial's job is to hold the formative view — to have the convictions that other people eventually catch up to.

01
Demand-shaping, not reflection
We shape demand. We don't reflect it.

India Editorial's role is not to surface what's already popular — it's to create the conditions for what becomes popular. The best editorial work happens ahead of the curve, not behind it. When a playlist becomes a cultural reference point, that's the job done right.

02
Programming, narrative, and ecosystem
The playlist is a container. What lives inside it is what matters.

Great editorial goes beyond curation into cultural authorship. The narrative, the voice, the context we build around a playlist — that's the product. A collection of tracks is a library. An editorial point of view is a statement.

03
Catalogue, language, and cultural context
India listens in layers. We programme the same way.

Sustained listening in India isn't driven by new releases alone. It lives in catalogue — in the regional identities, the music tied to memory, season, ceremony, and community. Hindi, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu are not interchangeable. Each is a distinct world. We programme them that way.

Direction · 3 min read

What We're
Changing.

Three shifts. Each one is a choice about where to place our editorial weight — and what to leave behind.

Release-led programming
Editorial calendar driven by what's dropping this week. More playlists, less meaning per playlist.
→
Intention-led programming
Fewer playlists, each one with a clear reason to exist. We programme with a purpose, not a release date. Quality of editorial judgement over quantity of surfaces.
Surface coverage
Treating language markets as segments to be served rather than worlds to be owned.
→
Language ecosystem ownership
Hindi, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu each get a dedicated editorial identity — not just localised content, but a genuine point of view about what that culture sounds like on Spotify.
Reactive storytelling
Amplifying cultural moments after they've already happened. Coverage, not creation.
→
Proactive cultural narrative
An editorial voice that anticipates moments and shapes conversations. We don't comment on culture — we participate in it, and sometimes we start it.

The Direction.

What these beliefs and shifts add up to — not a list of deliverables, but a view of where India Editorial is headed.

—
A team operating with editorial conviction. Every decision has a reason. Not just a reason to add — but a reason to leave things out.
—
Sharper prioritisation. The discipline to do less, better. Fewer surfaces with more meaning each.
—
A clear editorial identity for India that Spotify owns in the market. Not a brand that borrowed cultural credibility — one that earned it, playlist by playlist.
—
Language ecosystems that feel like destinations. Places listeners come back to — not because the algorithm sent them, but because they trust the voice.
